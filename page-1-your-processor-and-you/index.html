Page 1 - Your Processor and You

What Is A Processor? 

A Central Processing Unit, or CPU, is an essential component in a computer that is responsible for arithmetic, logical and input/output operations. Most definitions describe the CPU as the "brain" of the computer, but such a definition is too vague and rather useless. If we truly look under the plastic case of the CPU, we would see billions of transistors, physical wires, and memory caches located on the chip. Each of which is responsible for carrying out the numerous mathematical operations. Addition, subtraction, multiplication and division are the main arithmetic operations that a CPU performs. To make the CPU's job a bit more difficult, there are integer and floating point operation variants that require special consideration. In order to carry out these seemingly simple arithmetic operations the CPU must undergo several substeps which prepare, process and store the resulting data.

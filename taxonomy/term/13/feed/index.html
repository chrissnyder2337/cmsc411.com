In order to do so, you should run something called a "stress-test" for your CPU. A CPU stress test is a program which heavily utilizes CPU resources. For example, calculating many digits of pi or many prime numbers would utilize CPU resources. If your overclocked system-build runs reasonable operating tempertures after hours of heavy stress, then you can safetly assume that your CPU will not overheat when operating in similair (heavy stress) situations.</span></p> <p style="text-align: center;"><span style="line-height: 1.538em;"><img alt="" src="http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/0/07/Prime95_quad-core.png" /></span></p> <p style="text-align: center;"><span style="line-height: 1.538em;">(Prime 95 window - Running 4 Worker Threads)</span></p> <p><span style="line-height: 1.538em;">This is an example of a CPU stress test program called <strong>Prime 95</strong> - this program stresses the CPU immensely, Prime 95 is sometimes refered to as a "Torture Test".

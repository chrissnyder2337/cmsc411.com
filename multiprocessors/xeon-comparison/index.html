Xeon Comparison

This page gives a comparison of the different Xeon microprocessor families.

 

Xeon codenames do not refer to specific microprocessors. Instead, they refer to family of similar microprocessors. As such, attributes such as Frequency will be described as a range rather than as a specific value.

Xeon series numbers are used in early Xeon families:

  • 3--- series are meant for single-CPU operation, rather than for workstations or servers.
  • 5--- series are dual-core CPUs meant for use with servers and workstations.
  • 7--- series are meant for use with large servers and are multiprocessor-capable

After the release of "Westmere-EX," Intel started a new naming scheme:

  • 3--- series became E3----
  • 5--- series became E5----
  • 7--- series became E7----

In addition, codenames that contain "DP" denote dual-core, while "MP denotes multi-core.

History of Xbox

Original Xbox history:

 

The original Xbox was released November 15, 2001 in North America. It was Microsoft's first attempt at a home console. The Xbox competed with other 6th generation consoles such as the Sony PlayStation 2, the Nintendo Gamecube, and the Sega Dreamcast. It introduced Xbox live, and online gaming service. Although it was not free (like the PlayStation 2's and Dreamcast's online service was) it was a major success due to better severs, a friends list, and great online multiplayer titles such as Halo 2.

 

Original Xbox specs:

64 MB DDR SDRAM

733 MHz X86 Intel Celeron/Pentium III Custom Hybrid

 

 

Xbox 360 history:

 

The Xbox 360 is the second video game system developed by Microsoft. It was part of the 7th generation of video game consoles and competed with the Sony PlayStation 3, and Nintendo Wii. It's Xbox Live service allowed players to compete online, download games, TV shows, movies, and more. It also offers access to several multimedia streaming services.

 

Xbox 360 specs:

512 MB GDDR3 RAM

3.2 GHz IBM PowerPC tri-core

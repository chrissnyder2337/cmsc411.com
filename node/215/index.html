PSU

 

The Power Supply Unit (PSU) is the component that converts AC power to regulated DC power for use in the computer.

Things to consider when purchasing a PSU:

  • Will it fit in your case?

Like with every other component in a computer, if it does not fit in the case, it is useless.

  • How efficient is it?

If your machine is going to be running continuously, as with a server, efficiency is very important.  

  • How much noise does it make?

The louder your PSU, the louder your entire computer will be.  

  • How much heat does it generate?

The more heat your PSU generates, the more heat that will need to be dissipated.  Another thing to consider is that when overclocking the computer, you need to worry about the amount of heat generated.

  • How much wattage can it generate?

With overclocking a computer, the power consumption will increase.  If your wattage is only high enough to power the computer before overclocking, you will need to upgrade your PSU.

A wattage calculator can help you determine how much wattage is needed.  Here is one from Newegg: http://images10.newegg.com/BizIntell/tool/psucalc/index.html?name=Power-Supply-Wattage-Calculator

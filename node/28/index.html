Page 2 - RISC vs CISC, What's the Big Deal?

The information below will explain what CISC and RISC architectures are, why they were invented, why RISC has the upper-hand over CISC, and how these architectures are used today.

What is the CISC Architecture?
CISC, or Complex Instruction Set Computing, is a design strategy that works with a handful of instructions that are complex. What do we mean here by complex? It just means that the few instructions we are working with can execute several operations altogether, which makes them quite complex.

So, What is the RISC Architecture?
RISC, or Reduced Instruction Set Computing, is a design strategy that works with simplified instructions since they can provide higher performance. In other words, rather than working with instructions that are very complex and require several sub-calculations, the architecture works with instructions that are simpler and faster to execute. Since the instructions are so simple, RISC-based processors execute the instructions faster and improve overall performance. What is performance, you may ask? Simply put, performance is the amount of work accomplished compared to the time and resources used to accomplish the work.

Moreover, RISC uses load and store architecture where memory is only accessed through load or store operations and not operations like add or subtract. Additionally, for a load or store operation, data in memory must be stored in registers; at the end of the operation, the data must be stored back into memory.
